Impression, Sunrise

  • Artist: Claude Monet
  • Year: 1872
  • Style: Impressionism
  • Description: This work gave the Impressionist movement its name. It depicts the port of Le Havre in the early morning, with hazy outlines and soft colors. Monet's use of color and brushstrokes conveys fleeting impressions of light and atmosphere.

The Persistence of Memory

  • Artist: Salvador Dalí
  • Year: 1931
  • Style: Surrealism
  • Description: One of Dalí's most famous works, featuring melting clocks against a surreal landscape. The painting embodies surrealism’s exploration of the fluidity of time and the relativity of memory.

Mona Lisa

  • Artist: Salvador Dalí
  • Year: Approximately 1503–1506
  • Style: Renaissance
  • Description: This legendary Renaissance portrait is famous for its enigmatic smile and realism. The "Mona Lisa" combines portraiture with a natural background, emphasizing the harmony between humanity and nature.

No. 5, 1948

  • Artist: Jackson Pollock
  • Year: 1948
  • Style: Abstract Expressionism
  • Description: Created using the "drip" painting technique, with paint chaotically distributed across the canvas. Pollock’s work demonstrates emotion and energy, avoiding clear shapes and subjects. This painting became one of the most famous pieces in abstract expressionism, sparking much debate.
