Key traditions

  1. New Year and Christmas Celebrations
    • Homes are decorated, and traditional dishes are prepared.
    • People sing carols, visit churches, and celebrate with fireworks on New Yearâ€™s Eve, creating a festive atmosphere.
  2. Ivana Kupala
    • This summer holiday is celebrated in parks and nature areas.
    • People gather around bonfires, dance in circles, jump over the flames, and participate in the search for the mythical "fern flower."
  3. Maslenitsa
    • Maslenitsa marks the farewell to winter with vibrant outdoor celebrations.
    • People enjoy traditional games, music, and lots of pancakes, creating a lively, colorful festival.
  4. Independence Day
    • Independence Day is marked with parades, concerts, and cultural events.
    • Families gather to celebrate and honor the country's history and achievements.

Traditional dishes

  1. Kutia
    • A traditional Christmas dish made from wheat and honey, symbolizing prosperity and unity among family members.
    • Often mixed with poppy seeds, raisins, and nuts to add flavor and texture.
    • Served as the first dish during the Christmas meal, setting a symbolic start to the celebration.
  2. Blini (Pancakes)
    • Popular during Maslenitsa, symbolizing the sun and the end of winter.
    • Traditionally served with toppings such as honey, sour cream, jam, or caviar.
    • People gather to make and eat blini in large quantities, enjoying the festive atmosphere.
  3. Paskha (Easter Bread)
    • A special bread prepared for Easter, often decorated with religious symbols or colorful icing.
    • Made with rich ingredients like butter, eggs, and raisins, symbolizing abundance.
    • Shared with family and friends to celebrate renewal, hope, and the arrival of spring.
