Breaking Bad

The series "Breaking Bad", created by Vince Gilligan, first aired on AMC in January 2008. The story of its creation is very interesting and is connected with an unusual idea. The idea for the series came to Gilligan during a conversation with a friend. They discussed the failures in life and Gilligan joked, saying: "You should just start producing methamphetamine." This banal conversation attracted attention to the unusual idea - about a man who begins to engage in a criminal business. Gilligan developed the concept of the series, inspired by stories about people whose lives change dramatically due to unexpected circumstances. He presented the story of Walter White, a chemistry teacher who, having learned about his cancer, decides to engage in drug production to provide for his family. Despite the unusual idea, "Breaking Bad" faced difficulties in finding a channel for its release. However, after showing a prototype of the episode at a script conference, AMC showed interest and the series was given the green light.
